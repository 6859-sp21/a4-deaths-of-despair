Deaths of Despair

Visualizing Deaths by Age-Race Groups

In 2015, Anne Case and Angus Deaton showed that increases in drug overdoses, suicides, and drug-or-alcohol-related liver mortality
among middle aged whites between 1999 and 2013 challenged decades of decrease in US mortality. We revisit the data to see whether increases in these "deaths of despair" are still particular to white non-Hispanic Americans. Unforunately, our more recent findings (post 2015) show that increasing rates of deaths-of-despair have become prevalent across all races, with an unsettling 99% increase among Black populations.
